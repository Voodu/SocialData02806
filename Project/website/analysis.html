Pedestrian Activity and Accidents in Melbourne

Melbourne is the second most populous city in Australia. It is a home to many of Australia's well-known landmarks. It offers great food, amazing nightlife, cultural and music festivals, and a vibrant atmosphere right in the heart of the city. It is developing and offering more opportunities day by day.
Like this article says - "Walking is important to a city - pedestrian activity is an indication of a city's vibrancy and vitality. There is also a direct link between a city's economic prosperity and the safety and convenience of the pedestrian experience". Melbourne has various sensors installed throughout the city to record the pedestrian count of the places.
It helps it in finding ways to improve the city's walkability and transportation. Let's find out the most visited places and time periods when the streets are busiest. Safety also plays an important role in the liveability of a city. We'll also take a look at trends and analysis of road accidents that occur over the city.

Road Accidents

Average Road Crashes by Year


The number of accidents seemed to be rising except for a drop in 2017 and 2020. 
Because of lockdown and pandemic, there seems to be significant drop in 2020. 


Average Road Crashes by Month


The peaks during the march, May, and october can be caused because of melbourne's changing seasons.

  • Summer - December to February
  • Autumn - March to May
  • Winter - June to August
  • Spring - September to November

January is the hottest, people would be less eager to go out. Hence, lower number of accidents.
October is the wettest month in Melbourne, which could be the reason for the peak number of accidents.


Average Road Crashes by Week Day


The number of crashes seems to be increasing from Monday to Friday with a peak at Friday. And then a sudden drop in the weekends. 
People tend to have weekend parties on Fridays. So, there could be higher traffic or also people tend to be more irresponsible during Friday evenings.


Average Road Crashes by Hour


The most number of accidents occur during morning peak hours (from 8 A.M to 9 A.M) and the evening peak hours (from 5 P.M to 6 P.M)
It could be because of the high traffic during these times.

How would Melbourne look like without COVID-19?

The effects of COVID-19 

Melbourne is affected by global pandemic just like any other place in world, and it can definitely be seen in the number of pedestrians. The following figure shows how new COVID-19 cases have affected the number ofpedestrians

Predicting a world without the COVID-19 using weather data

The pandemic sucks! But how would the world look like without the Pandemic? We can use try to predict how the number of pedestrians would have looked like withoutany lockdowns or social distancing.

There are some clear patterns, which is also described in the section “Pedestrians”. The data contains more than 3.2 million observations, which is plenty of data to start doing some Machine Learning!

By including data about the weather from the Australian Bureau of Metrology, from the past 10 years, we can potentially increase the accuracy of our predictions. The weather data includes information about rainfall, temperature, and the solar exposure. The following plot shows a clear indication of some seasonal patterns, that will be great for improving our model.

A world without COVID-19

We choose to use random forest regression, which is an ensemble method of decision trees. By splitting our data into a test set and a validation set, we could estimate the error of the model. Our predicted error is MSE=194 with an accuracy of 0.94 R^2.

We can also investigate how the model estimate the number of pedestrians at a specific
location. The following plots shows that our predictions follow the same locational patterns as the real observations, but with a much lower amount!
